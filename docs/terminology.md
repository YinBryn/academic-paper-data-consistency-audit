# Terminology

This document defines the key terms and concepts used within the technical audit framework.

---

## 1. Technical Audit (技术核查)
A systematic, objective evaluation of the data, calculations, and claims presented in a published scientific article. Unlike typical peer reviews, which focus on novelty and high-level interpretation, a technical audit focuses on the internal consistency of the datasets and the correctness of basic mathematical and physical calculations.

## 2. Reproducibility Check (可复现性检查)
An evaluation to verify if the secondary, calculated, or fitted values reported in a paper (e.g., activation energies, statistical means, standard deviations, fit quality metrics) can be mathematically recreated. This is done using only the raw or tabular data provided in the article and the standard physical equations disclosed in the text.

## 3. Data Consistency (数据自洽性)
The requirement that the same physical parameter or property under identical conditions must match across all parts of a paper. This includes the Abstract, main text body, figures, tables, and Supplementary Information (SI).

## 4. Physics-Informed Check (物理合理性检查)
Using the fundamental laws of thermodynamics, kinetics, and mass/charge conservation to assess the feasibility of reported experimental values and transport parameters. For instance, checking whether the reported power density is the product of current and voltage, or if a diffusion coefficient exceeds the physical limits of solid-state lattice diffusion.

## 5. Evidence–Claim Alignment (结论-证据匹配度)
An audit of the logical connection between experimental data and the qualitative statements made by the authors. This check ensures that conclusions, particularly regarding mechanism or performance superiority, do not overshoot the actual resolution or scope of the provided empirical evidence.

## 6. Post-Publication Technical Comment (出版后技术评论)
A formal, neutral, and scientifically focused communication addressed to journal editors or published directly in response to a paper. Its goal is to raise questions about data discrepancies or request clarification on calculations, thereby maintaining the accuracy and reliability of the scientific record.
