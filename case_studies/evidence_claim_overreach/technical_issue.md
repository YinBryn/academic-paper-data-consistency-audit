# Technical Issue: Evidence-Claim Alignment

## Location

Synthetic mechanistic discussion paragraph.

## Observation

The synthetic claim states that improved fuel-cell performance is caused by faster proton transport through the bulk lattice.

The available synthetic evidence includes improved I–V performance, lower total polarization resistance, unchanged major XRD phase, and similar SEM morphology.

## Check / Reasoning

The evidence supports a performance improvement but does not uniquely identify faster bulk proton transport as the causal mechanism. Alternative or additional contributors may include:

- reduced electrode polarization resistance
- improved electrode/electrolyte interface contact
- altered gas diffusion or surface exchange
- different microstructure or effective active area
- changes in hydration or defect chemistry not directly measured in the synthetic example

Evidence that would more directly support the stated mechanism could include proton conductivity measurements, isotope-effect tests, transport-number measurements, hydration thermodynamics, or controlled separation of bulk and interfacial contributions.

## Why It Matters

Mechanistic claims are often used to guide material design. If the evidence only supports improved performance but not the proposed causal pathway, the conclusion may need to be narrowed or additional evidence may be required.

## Evidence Level

L4 — evidence-claim overextension.
