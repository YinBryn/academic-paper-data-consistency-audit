# Neutral Comment Draft

I would appreciate clarification regarding the mechanistic statement that the improved fuel-cell performance is caused by faster bulk proton transport.

The synthetic evidence appears to show improved performance and lower total polarization resistance. However, I could not identify direct measurements that isolate bulk proton transport, such as proton conductivity, isotope-effect tests, transport-number measurements, or hydration thermodynamics.

Could the authors clarify whether additional evidence supports the assignment of the performance improvement specifically to faster bulk proton transport, rather than to electrode polarization, interfacial contact, microstructure, or other kinetic contributions?
